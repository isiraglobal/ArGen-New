Cookie Policy

Effective Date: May 6, 2026. Last Updated: May 6, 2026.

ArGen ("we," "us," or "our") uses cookies, web beacons, and similar tracking technologies on our website at argen.isira.club (the "Website") and within our AI performance evaluation platform (the "Services"). This Cookie Policy explains what these technologies are, why we use them, and how you can control their usage. By continuing to browse or use our Website and Services, you consent to our use of cookies as described in this policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently and provide information to the website owners. Cookies enable a website to remember your actions and preferences (such as login state, language, and display settings) over a period of time, so you do not have to re-enter them each time you return to the site or navigate from one page to another.

In addition to cookies, we may also use local storage and session storage (collectively, "browser storage") for similar purposes, particularly for persisting authentication tokens and user interface preferences across your browsing session.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

We categorize the cookies and similar technologies used on our platform into the following types based on their function and necessity:

Strictly Necessary Cookies (Essential): These cookies are essential for the operation of our Website and Services. Without these cookies, certain features you have requested, such as secure login access to your ArGen dashboard, session management, and navigation through protected areas of the platform, would not be possible. These cookies do not gather information about you for marketing purposes and cannot be disabled through our systems. Examples include the argen_token authentication cookie and session identifiers.

Functional and Preference Cookies: These cookies enable the Website to remember choices you make (such as your preferred language, timezone, or the region you are in) and provide enhanced, more personalized features. For instance, we may store your dashboard display preferences (e.g., dark mode toggle, evaluation metric groupings) using a preference cookie so that your settings persist across sessions.

Analytics and Performance Cookies: These cookies allow us to count visits and traffic sources so we can measure and improve the performance of our Website and platform. They help us understand which pages are the most and least popular, how users navigate through the site, and whether users encounter errors. All information these cookies collect is aggregated and therefore anonymized. If you do not allow these cookies, we will not know when you have visited our site and will not be able to monitor its performance effectively.

3. Third-Party Cookies

In addition to our own first-party cookies, we engage trusted third-party service providers who may set cookies on your device when you visit our Website. These third parties are contractually obligated to use your data only for the purposes specified by us and in compliance with applicable data protection laws. The specific third parties we work with include:

  • Vercel Analytics: Our hosting provider, Vercel, deploys its own speed analytics and performance cookies to monitor the availability, load times, and error rates of our platform. These cookies help us ensure the Website remains fast and reliable for all users. Vercel's data collection is governed by their privacy policy and does not include personal identifiers.
  • Google Analytics: We use Google Analytics to understand aggregate traffic patterns and user engagement with our content. Google Analytics uses its own set of cookies (e.g., _ga, _gid) to distinguish unique users and throttle request rates. The information generated by these cookies about your use of the Website (including your truncated IP address) is transmitted to and stored by Google on servers in the United States. We have enabled IP anonymization to mask your full IP address before any storage or processing takes place. Google's use of the data is subject to their privacy policy.

4. How to Manage and Control Cookies

You have the right to decide whether to accept or reject cookies. You can exercise your cookie preferences by adjusting your browser settings. Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings preferences. Below is a general guide for managing cookies on common browsers:

  • Google Chrome: Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies and other site data.
  • Mozilla Firefox: Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data.
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Cookies and website data.
  • Microsoft Edge: Settings → Site permissions → Cookies and site data.

Please note that if you choose to disable or reject cookies through your browser settings, some parts of the ArGen Website and Services may become inaccessible or may not function properly. Specifically, disabling essential session cookies will prevent you from logging into your account or accessing any protected features of the platform.

5. Do Not Track Signals

Your browser may allow you to transmit a "Do Not Track" ("DNT") signal to indicate that you do not wish to have your online activity tracked. Our Website does not currently respond to DNT signals because there is no industry standard for how websites should interpret them. However, you can manage your cookie preferences as described in Section 4 above to limit tracking on our platform.

6. Updates to This Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, applicable laws, or operational requirements. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last Updated" date. We encourage you to review this Cookie Policy periodically to stay informed about how we use cookies and similar technologies. If we make material changes, we will notify you via email or through a prominent notice on our Website.
